YouTube channel and parallel Canvas OER course teaching foundational human competencies — "sovereign humans first, tools second." Not anti-AI, pro-agency.

**Why:** Students (and everyone) need to understand skills before outsourcing them to tools. Builds human agency as a prerequisite to automation.

**How to apply:** This project shares DNA with MULTI 211 — same Horton Absorb-Do-Connect framework, same 6-part level template, same tone/reading level targets, same CVC-OEI compliance. Treat it as a sibling project when making design decisions.

## Delivery
- **YouTube** — free, episodic, public-facing
- **Canvas OER** — structured course with assessment and reflection
- UDL-aligned, CVC-OEI compatible

## Topic Areas
- **Digital Survival:** passwords, 3-2-1 backups, phishing, follow-the-money media literacy
- **Navigation & Orientation:** paper maps, topo maps, reading a room
- **Critical Listening & Watching:** active music listening, critical film watching, reading images
- **Scripting & Automation:** scripting logic (thinking course, not coding), personal workflows, when to automate
- **Critical Thinking:** source evaluation, fact vs. interpretation vs. opinion
- **Cultural Literacy:** underrecognized figures (Dolores Huerta case study), demanding music genres, why difficulty in art matters

## Anchor Examples
- **Music:** Oscar Peterson Trio, *Live in Chicago*, 1960 — music that asks something of you
- **Cultural:** Dolores Huerta — erased labor, politics of credit
