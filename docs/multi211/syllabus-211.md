# MULTI 211 Syllabus Reference

Source: Course_Syllabus_Adobe_Photoshop_II.pdf

## Course Info
- Instructor: Mike Walter
- College: Crafton Hills College
- Semester: Spring 2025 (Feb 18 – May 22)
- Prerequisites: MULTI 111
- Credits: 3
- Delivery: Fully online, weekly recorded lectures
- Required Software: Photoshop, Bridge, Media Encoder

## Course Objectives
- Produce artwork based on professional design standards
- Create compositions with enhanced blend modes and masking tools
- Define and implement automated tasks and actions
- Produce video and animated sequences
- Develop digital painting techniques for media production

## Student Learning Outcomes
- Communicate using digital media industry terms
- Solve design challenges using critical and creative thinking
- Demonstrate sensitivity to ethical thinking in media production
- Research and develop visual planning strategies
- Demonstrate understanding of digital production requirements

## Topics Covered (Weekly Sequence)
1. Course intro, ethics, image concepts, Bridge, Photoshop preferences and navigation
2. Camera settings, raw formats, Camera Raw Filter, HDR, luminosity masks
3. Retouching review, neural filters, restoration, frequency separation
4. Blending modes review, advanced layer style blending, colorization
5. Spring Break
6. Selection/masking review, refinement for fine details, channel masking,
   color range, pen tool/vector masks
7. Generative AI compositing, replacing objects/backgrounds, color matching
8. Digital sketching/painting, drawing tablets, custom brushes, patterns, swatches
9. History/art history brushes, mixer brush, digital painting
10. Vector tools, character/paragraph styles, custom fonts, text shapes
11. Research/planning, composition/layout, layer comps, artboards, frames
12. Video editing, keyframing, animation, video effects compositing
13. Actions, batch processing, image stacks, data-driven graphics
14. Final project

## Grading
- Standard letter grade (A–F)
- Class Projects: 20 pts each
- Homework Projects: 40 pts each
- Final Project: 100 pts
- Late work: 10% deduction within 1 week; 50% after 1 week
